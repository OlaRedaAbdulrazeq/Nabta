special Flower

Tulips are a genus of 100 species of flowering plants in the family Liliaceae. It is native to Europe, the Maghreb, Asia, Anatolia, Iran, eastern and northeastern China and Japan.
The flowers are usually large, showy, and brightly colored, usually red, pink, yellow, or white (usually in warm tones).
Planting occurs in late summer and early fall, and blooms in spring. Tulips grow in temperate regions that tend to be cold, and are distinguished by their ease of cultivation and their ability to withstand the severe cold of winter.
Tulips are perennial flowers, meaning they live for more than two years. Tulips are grown from bulbs, which are grains that resemble an onion plant in terms of shape. Tulips of all kinds grow on stems ranging from 10 to 70 centimeters in length. The flowers bear between two and 6 green leaves that appear to be covered with wax, including types that bear 12 leaves.
